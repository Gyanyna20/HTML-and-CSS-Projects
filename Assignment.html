Intresting facts about cactus

Cacti can store large amounts of water in their stems, allowing them to survive months (sometimes years!) without rain.
Their spines are actually modified leaves, which help reduce water loss and protect them from animals.
Some cacti produce huge, beautiful flowers that bloom only at night and last just a few hours.
Cacti don’t all live in deserts—some species grow in rainforests and mountains.
The tallest cactus, the saguaro, can grow over 40 feet (12 meters) tall and live for more than 150 years.
Cacti use a special process called CAM photosynthesis, opening their pores at night to save water.
Certain cacti, like prickly pear, are edible and used in food, medicine, and cosmetics.
